Solutions/Actions to the Problem

The Philippines has a large drug population and a thriving drug trade. However, almost two years into the government's anti-drug effort, there is no evidence that the exclusive concentration on enforcement-led and punitive policies is effective. It's also likely that tough-on-crime enforcement is squeezing out more sustainable and effective approaches to drug recovery, such as health- and community-based initiatives.

These are the viable solutions that I could contribute in this website

  • 2 tsp Canola or Sesame Oil
  • 1 (10-oz) package Frozen Broccoli (or stir-fry vegetable mix), thawed*
  • 1/2 C stir-fry Sauce
  • 1/4 C Pineapple Juice, use reserved juice from Canned Pineapple
  • 1/4 tsp Garlic Powder
  • 1/4 tsp Crushed Red Pepper (optional - use if you like it hot)
  • 1 (15 oz) Canned Pineapple chunks or tidbits, drained (reserve the juice)
  • 2 C diced Cooked Chicken or 2(10-oz) Cans of Chicken Breast (drained and flaked)

    • Method of Cooking

    1. Heat the oil in a large skillet over medium high heat. Add all ingredients EXCEPT the pineapple & chicken. Cook and stir until heated through, 5-6 mins
    2. Add pineapple and chicken; cook another 2 minutes. Serve over instant brown rice or whole wheat pasta
    3. Thaw frozen vegetables in the microwave or by holding the package under cold running water for several minutes
